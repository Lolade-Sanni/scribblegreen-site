About the Programme

What Is the Scribble Green Ambassador Programme?

The Scribble Green Ambassador Programme connects motivated students with real professional opportunities at one of Nigeria's most innovative research and writing firms. As an ambassador, you become our official representative on your campus — spreading the word about our events, resources, and courses to fellow students and faculty.

But it's far more than promotion. You get access to exclusive training, mentorship from our senior team, and a platform to build the kind of professional record that genuinely impresses employers and academic institutions alike.

We're looking for students who are curious, proactive, and genuinely invested in helping peers access quality knowledge and skills — regardless of department or discipline.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

help_outline How many ambassadors do you recruit per university?
add

We typically aim for one to three ambassadors per campus, depending on the size of the institution and current programme demand. In some cases we recruit a lead ambassador who then builds a small team beneath them — this is a good opportunity for students interested in leadership roles.

help_outline How long is the ambassador term?
add

The initial term is one academic semester. At the end of each term, ambassadors in good standing are invited to renew for another semester. There is no maximum term length — some ambassadors have been with us since the programme launched. Renewal is based on engagement and performance, not automatic.

help_outline What does "representing Scribble Green on campus" actually involve?
add

Activities vary per ambassador and campus, but generally include: sharing Scribble Green content with peers via social media and WhatsApp; promoting upcoming events and courses; organising or attending information sessions; and providing feedback to the Scribble Green team about the kinds of support students on your campus actually need. You won't be cold-selling anything — the focus is education and community-building, not sales.

help_outline Can I be an ambassador if I'm in my final year?
add

Yes, absolutely. We actively recruit final-year students — your campus connections are often wider, and the professional benefits (reference letters, course access, mentorship) are especially timely as you approach graduation. You are eligible for the full programme regardless of year group.

help_outline How long does the application process take?
add

From submission to decision typically takes 5–10 business days. Shortlisted candidates are contacted within that window for their interview. We aim to be respectful of your time — if you haven't heard from us within two weeks of submitting, feel free to follow up at m.johnscribblegreen@gmail.com.

help_outline Is there a performance target I have to hit?
add

There are light activity expectations — attending monthly check-ins, sharing content regularly, and completing at least one campus activation per term. These are agreed during onboarding and are genuinely designed to be achievable alongside full-time study. We don't demand quotas or make the programme feel like a second job. If life gets busy, we'll work with you.

help_outline My university is not in Nigeria — can I still apply?
add

The current programme is focused on Nigerian universities, but we are actively planning to expand to other African countries in the next intake cycle. If you are based outside Nigeria and want to be considered for an upcoming cohort, submit an interest form via our contact page, and we'll reach out when your region opens.
